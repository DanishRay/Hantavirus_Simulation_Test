Simulation Findings Log

Virions Travel Time :-
The virus particles (virions) move from the infected source cell to the healthy target cell over a period of 72 hours (approx.. 3.0 days)

Incubation Phase :-
The Exposed state occurs as soon as the particles touch the target cell radius.
The transition from the exposed phase to the infectious (shedding) phase happens very quickly.
The simulation reaches the final infectious state almost immediately after the first contact.

Clock and Timeline Behavior :- 
The simulation clock stops as soon as the target cell becomes infectious
The final recorded simulation time reflects the point when the cell begins shedding new virus particles

Model Acknowledgements

Visual Representation :- 
The model uses a simple 2D graph to represent the environment
Cells are represented as circles, and virions are represented as small dots

The model is a basic simulation and does not contain high-level biological details

Simulation Intent :-
The purpose of this model is to show the process of how one cell infects another nearby cell
It tracks the movement of particles and the change in the state of the target cell
